## Phase I — Avenue Homes Recognition

> Sponsorships at the gala-table level cost $5,000–$25,000 and presume the buyer has a brand worth attaching to a charity's name. Avenue Homes hasn't earned that yet — and that's fine. The right move is to build recognition through *presence*, not purchase.

**Total Phase 1 budget:** ~$2,000 over nine months.

### The Hierarchy of Visibility

| Step | Stage | Cost |
|------|-------|------|
| **1** *(now)* | Personal volunteer presence | $0 — time only |
| **2** | Branded community events | $100–$500 each |
| **3** | Small local sponsorships | $300–$2,000 each |
| **4** | Charity gala sponsorships | $5,000–$25,000 *(Phase II)* |
| **5** | Major institutional giving | $50,000+ *(Phase III+)* |

### Personal Volunteer Identity

Pick three. Show up consistently. The sponsorship invitations follow.

- **Habitat for Humanity Tampa** — real estate adjacent; Build Days are public and immediately legible to clients
- **Metropolitan Ministries** — Holiday Tent in Nov–Dec is the city's largest volunteer event
- **Junior League of Tampa** — Holiday Gift Market accepts non-member volunteers; volunteer hours strengthen later application
- **Tampa Bay Watch** — Davis Islands area; estuary cleanup, oyster reef restoration
- **Keep Tampa Bay Beautiful** — Bayshore corridor cleanup days
- **Feeding Tampa Bay** — easy group volunteer shifts for an Avenue Homes team
- **The Spring of Tampa Bay** — domestic violence shelter; women's giving network
- **Plant High School Boosters** — single-team uniform sponsorship, ~$300–500
- **Salvation Army Red Kettle** — December bell-ringing in branded apron

## Phase III — The Continental Bridge

> Translate Tampa standing into transatlantic visibility. Two or three professional anchors per year, two or three cultural ones, and consistent presence at two or three institutions you genuinely care about.

### The Eight Movements

1. **A real reason to be there** — international real-estate partnership (Knight Frank / Engel & Völkers / Sotheby's International)
2. **The cultural calendar** — memorise it, attend it; begin with the accessible tier
3. **Philanthropy as passport** — junior committee or young patrons board of a European institution
4. **Sport in the European register** — equestrian, sailing, polo, tennis, skiing; choose one
5. **Education as accelerator** — INSEAD, IMD, Oxford Saïd, HEC, Bocconi
6. **Languages** — French and Italian to true fluency; conversational doesn't open the door
7. **Personal presentation** — quietly expensive; restraint reads as old money
8. **Realistic timeline** — two to three years across two or three avenues, not all eight at once
